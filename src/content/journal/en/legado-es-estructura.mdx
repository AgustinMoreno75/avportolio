Many people imagine legacy as reputation. But reputation is unstable if it is not backed by structure. What truly remains are transmitted habits, embodied principles, inheritable systems and useful institutions.

The important question is not whether you will be remembered, but what will keep working better because you were here. That is a much more demanding and much nobler metric.

## Design for what comes after

Building legacy requires thinking beyond the gratification of the present. It forces you to design for decades, not for short applause.

A serious legacy leaves tools, language, example and direction. It leaves a framework others can inhabit with more order and more strength.

## What remains

A purposeful life does not only accumulate. It organizes. It elevates. It transmits.

That is why legacy deserves rigor: it is the part of your work that will keep teaching when you are no longer there to explain it.
